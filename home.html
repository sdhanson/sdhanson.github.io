WIP Research
During Summer 2018, I conducted research in Vanderbilt University's Learning in Virtual Environments Lab. My focus was navigation for mobile virtual reality devices, specifically the GearVR and Oculus GO.
Over 3 months, I built 8 algorithms that translate real life walking-in-place phone accelerometer data into steps in the virtual world, allowing users to move in a 3 DOF VR system without moving in real life. Various aspects of the algorithms were individualized to the user.
The most interesting algorithm utilized a TensorFlow Convolutional Neural Network to recognize human activity. I integrated this algorithm into Unity (read about the process on Medium ).
